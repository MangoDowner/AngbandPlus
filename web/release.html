Release: Lepton (v0.1)

The Lepton-release is the first non-developer relase, but it is a concept-release. That means "See, this concept works and is on the right track, and now we're picking up steam." It is not meant to be a complete playable version, it's a v0.1 release that shows that the project is alive and has realisable plans to reach a goal.

The Lepton-release will show that basic features of the goal of an engine that supports pluggable angband-variants are implemented or implementable. It will not have all features, nor all features fully implemented.

The v0.1 release has many of the most used commands and functionality. Focus has been on the most important and most used functions, and those you meet early in the game. Some basic principles in development:

  • Focus on objects and monsters found before 1000' and their abilities and powers.
  • No fancy features, e.g visuals.
  • No extended features when a simpler solution exists, e.g no point-based stat-system, but simple autoroller, and no haggling, but fixed prices.
  • Don't implement features not strictly needed, e.g target-code, hitpoints-warnings, ...

Things that are included

  • Most commands (eat, drop, drink, pick up, read, zap wand, ...).
  • Basic fighting and basic spells
  • Most common objects with effects
  • Doors, traps, corridors and rooms in the dungeon
  • Graphical tiles for X11, *including* images (which is not in any other angband variant)
  • Aggressive monsters and nasty combat attacks by monsters.
  • Bows, wands, staves and rods and a fireball for your enemies.
  • Simple stores, a simple home and quick to create a character.
Things that will not be included:
  • vaults or pits
  • artifacts
  • monster-powers commonly found below 1000' (some exceptions)
  • object-powers commonly found below 1000' (some exceptions)
  • spellbooks below 1000' (some exceptions)
  • store restock, restricted buy in shops
  • day/night in town
  • integrated room-code
  • ego-effects of below 1000'
  • monster use items or monsters pick up items
  • no monster-memory
  • perfect descriptions or perfect screens
  • character-dumps to text-files
  • a 'D'estroy command
  • targeting, running or resting commands
  • no point-based birth or haggling
  • refill of lamps or spiking of doors
  • perfect division of jobs between variant-code and engine
  • fully integrated event-code
  • home in town is not saved, also some other missing features in save/load

Quickly summed up, this means that a lot of things still need to be added and improved. I hope I'll be able to do this, but some help would be appreciated.
